01 / About

Coach, clinician, former athlete.

Stephan built Precision PT the way he built his own game — one honest rep at a time. No crowded gym floor, no cookie‑cutter worksheets. Just a clinician who's been on the court and under the pressure, now helping you get back there too.

From varsity hardwood to the treatment table.

Stephan grew up in the gym — varsity basketball through high school, countless early mornings and longer nights. The game taught him how bodies adapt when you ask the right thing of them, and how quickly a single bad movement pattern can sideline the rest of the season.

These days he's just as likely to be chasing a good lie on the back nine. Golf became his proving ground as a clinician — the same small, repeatable details that decide a swing decide whether a knee, hip or shoulder holds up over time. Precision PT is built on that idea: the details you can feel, taught until they're yours.

04 / Process

What a visit looks like.

A first session at Precision PT isn't a fifteen‑minute triage between two other patients. It's a full hour of undivided attention, a hands‑on assessment, and a plan you walk out of the room actually understanding.

01 00 : 00 — 00 : 15

Story first.

Before any test, we talk. What hurts, when it started, what you've already tried, and — the part most clinics skip — what you actually want to get back to.

02 00 : 15 — 00 : 35

Assess the whole chain.

The pain point rarely travels alone. Stephan assesses movement, strength, and load from the ground up until the real culprit shows itself.

03 00 : 35 — 00 : 55

Hands‑on & move.

Manual work, dry needling where it earns its place, then coached movement so you feel the change before you leave the room.

04 00 : 55 — 01 : 00

Walk away with a plan.

A short list of the right homework, a realistic timeline, and a cadence for follow‑ups. You'll know what comes next and why.

Precision PT does not bill insurance directly. Most PPO plans reimburse 50–80% of out‑of‑network PT — Stephan will give you an itemized superbill after each visit with the CPT and ICD codes your insurer needs. Patients with an HSA or FSA can pay directly from those accounts.
